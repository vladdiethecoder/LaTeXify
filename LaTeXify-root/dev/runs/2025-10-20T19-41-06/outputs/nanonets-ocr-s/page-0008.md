b) $g(x) = f(x + 1) - 3$

The $d = -1$ (since $x - d = x - (-1) = x + 1$) indicates a horizontal translation of 1 unit to the left and the $c = -3$ indicates a vertical translation of 3 units down.

$$x' = \frac{x}{k} + d$$
$$= \frac{1}{1} + (-1)$$
$$= 0$$

$$y' = a y + c$$
$$= 1(-2) + (-3)$$
$$= -5$$

Therefore, the resulting point is $\left(0, -5\right)$.

c) $g(x) = -f(2x)$

The $a = -1$ indicates a reflection in the $x$-axis and the $k = 2$ indicates a horizontal compression by a factor of $1/2$.

$$x' = \frac{x}{k} + d$$
$$= \frac{1}{2} + 0$$
$$= \frac{1}{2}$$

$$y' = a y + c$$
$$= (-1)(-2) + 0$$
$$= 2$$

Therefore, the resulting point is $\left(\frac{1}{2}, 2\right)$.
